# PMDD Demo Corpus: Scientific Evolution

## Section 1: Traditional Methodology
Scientific research in the late 20th century relied heavily on localized laboratory observations. 
The standard protocol for data collection was manual entry into physical logbooks. 
Researchers often published findings in specialized print journals with limited circulation.
The 'protocol' was seen as a rigid set of instructions that must be followed to maintain empirical validity.
Statistical significance was the primary metric for truth in these early experiments.

## Section 2: Digital Transformation
The 'protocol' has now shifted from a physical logbook to an automated digital pipeline.
Modern scientists utilize cloud computing to store petabytes of experimental data.
Open-source journals have replaced the traditional print model, favoring rapid dissemination.
Algorithms now handle the 'protocol' automatically, reducing human error in the laboratory.
The semantic shift of 'protocol' from a manual rulebook to a digital code base is a key example of linguistic drift.
This drift reflects the broader ideological change from human-centric to machine-assisted science.
